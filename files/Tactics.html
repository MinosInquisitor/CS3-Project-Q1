Tactics

The Fork: Bishop to g4
The Discovered Attack: Bishop to f4
The Pin

Tactics are dynamic moves that are made to take advantage of a short-term situation.

The fork is a move where a piece or a pawn attacks two pieces at the same time. Forks are often associated with knights, but in fact, every chess piece, including the pawn, is capable of forking other pieces. Forking an enemy king and with another piece is the most effective type of fork because the king will be in check and will be forced to move, leaving the other piece to be captured.

Discovered attacks are created when a piece is moved and a piece lying behind the piece that moves attacks an enemy piece. Think of it as an ambush. Discovered attacks are extremely effective against queens, rooks, and kings.

A pin occurs when a piece is attacked an cannot move because if it moves a more valuable piece behind it will be taken. An absolute pin, the most effective form of the pin, occurs when a piece cannot move because if it moves the king will be in check, which of course, is illegal.
