The Knight

Knight Checkmate

The knight (♘, ♞) is a piece in the game of chess represented by a horse's head and neck. It may move two squares vertically, one square horizontally, and one square vertically, jumping over other pieces. Knights capture enemy pieces by replacing them on their square.

Knights are slow, so they can only move slowly from one side of the board to the other. As a result, knights are vulnerable to threats from pawns, and pawns can quickly drive knights back (unless the knight finds a safe square that a pawn can't attack.)

Unlike a bishop, a knight is often not as good in an endgame. A knight can only exert control over one part of the board at a time and usually takes multiple moves to reposition to a new location, which often makes it less suitable in endgames with pawns on both sides of the board.
