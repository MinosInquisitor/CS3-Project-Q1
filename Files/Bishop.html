The Bishop


Bishops (♗, ♝) are long-range pieces that should place on long open diagonals. Unlike the knight, the bishop doesn't necessarily need an outpost in the center of the board - it can exert influence on the center from the edge. The best position for a bishop is one where it commands an open diagonal, and enemy pawns or knights cannot attack.

They are most potent in open positions, meaning pawns do not block the center of the board. However, one of the bishop's negatives is that a bishop must remain in one color. As a result, the bishop can sometimes render the bishop relatively useless in certain endgames. For example, the two knights were no match for the two bishops on an open board!

Bishop Strategies

• Bishops excel in open positions. If you have a bishop versus a knight, trade as many pieces as possible, except the bishop of course.

• Bishops need to be placed on diagonals that are not blocked to be effective.

• The value of the bishop tends to increase over the value of a Knight as more pieces are removed from the board.

• Having a pair of bishops versus two knights is generally an advantage.

• As most pieces, the bishop is most powerful in the center of the board.
